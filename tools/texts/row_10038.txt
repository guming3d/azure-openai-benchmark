Steve Wallis, an archaeologist at . Dorset County Council, said radio carbon dating showed the remains come . from about 970 to 1025. He said those dates fell within the period of Viking raids and atrocities on the Anglo-Saxons in Britain. Isotope analysis of teeth found in a severed jaw suggests they were from the Nordic countries. Mr Wallis said: 'It's great that the . burial pit is surprising us and teaching us more about who these men may . have been and what they may have been like.
