Twisted Sifter reports that the 2014 version of the costume had the LED strips hooked up to an Arduino microcontroller. The microcontroller is responsive to sound and has a button in the back of one of the ears along with two potentiometers and a microphone in the front of the costume. The outfit has 372 LEDS and 12 different modes. The vibrant costume is a hit with kids and parents alike as it's a sure-fire way to spot one's child in a crowd of costumed trick-or-treaters.
