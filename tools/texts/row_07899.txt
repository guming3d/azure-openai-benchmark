The word "hamsa" is derived from the Arabic word for "five," representing the fingers of the hand. The hamsa is often depicted as an open right hand with an eye in the palm, believed to provide protection and good fortune.  In Jewish and Kabbalistic traditions, the hamsa is known as the Hand of Miriam, representing the sister of Moses and symbolizing femininity and strength. It is believed to ward off the Evil Eye, a curse that causes misfortune or injury to an unsuspecting person.
